[1:00 - ARCHITECTURE]
(show assets/architecture_diagram.png)
Let me walk you through how this works under the hood. 
First, the camera logs enter the Security Checkpoint node, which scrubs out names or license plates and blocks prompt injections.
Next, the ok inputs are routed to our main orchestrator_agent, which reads the alert and classifies it.
Depending on the classification, the route_decision node forwards the request to either the ranger_threat_agent for security concerns, or the ecological_sightings_agent for wildlife logs.
Both agents can query our MCP Server on the right to retrieve conservation status, light conditions, or coordinates.
If the ranger_threat_agent detects a critical poaching risk, the flow pauses at the ranger_approval_node, where we wait for a human operator to approve dispatching rangers before generating the final_response.
